About me:
    I am currently pursuing an engineering degree (M.Sc.) from the Norwegian University of Science and Technology (NTNU) and my interests include applied mathematics and geophysics in the petroleum and minings sectors. I plan to write my masters thesis on applied geophysics and mathematics in the off-shore mining industry, more specifically on the possibility of using swarm technology to explore mineral resources in the vicinity of Mid-Ocean Ridges(MORs).
    I am very interested in the possibilities of resource recovery through deep sea mining and unconventional petroleum reservoirs, like the ones in the arctic, and the special technological requirements and challenges related to the exploration and production of these.
    Currently I’m working towards taking my 4th year abroad and have been granted the Fulbright scholarship for educational exchange in order to achieve this. I enjoy playing basketball, playing the guitar and participating in engineering and academic competitions, both individually and along with my classmates at NTNU.
